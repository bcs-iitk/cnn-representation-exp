About this experiment

The experiment consists of 7140 randomly sequenced trials. You are required to perform a minimum of 15 trials before you can submit the experiment. However, given the large scale of this experiment, we do not impose an upper limit on the number of trials you can perform. That is to say, beyond 15, you are free to do as many trials as you want, as long as you are doing them honestly. In particular, we urge you to do atleast 50 trials as it would be really helpful for our study.

In each trial, you will be shown two images of animals along with a list of features. For each feature, you will have to judge the amount of similarity in the two images on a scale of -5 to 5 with -5 being "Not similar at all" or "Very Dissimilar" and 5 being "Very Similar". However, note that your judgement should be based only on the images and not on your general perception of the animals in those images. To account for those cases when some features are not visible in the images, we have also provided a "Can't judge" option. Read the instructions page carefully to understand how to rate different features and how to procced to next trials and submit the experiment.

Technical requirements

The experiment is designed such that it can be performed on any screen. However, we recommend using a laptop or any bigger screen so that there is no disturbance in the stimulus.
